Raghupathi Reddy "snips a play"

MODESTY by Paul Hervieu

JACQUES. What have you against me? HENRIETTE. Nothing that you're to blame for. It is merely the fault of my character; _that_ forces me to refuse you. JACQUES. But I can't see why you----? HENRIETTE. [_With an air of great importance._] A great change is taking place in the hearts of us women. We have resolved henceforward not to be treated as dolls, but as creatures of reason. As for me, I am most unfortunate, for nobody ever did anything but flatter me. I have always been too self-satisfied, too---- JACQUES. You have always been the most charming of women, the most---- HENRIETTE. Stop! It's exactly that sort of exaggeration that's begun to make me so unsure of myself. I want you to understand once for all, Jacques, I have a conscience, and, furthermore, it is beginning to develop. I have taken some important resolutions. JACQUES. What _do_ you mean?
